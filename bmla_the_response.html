Her Claim: "Zero Complaints Have Been Upheld"
Unverifiable
▼
What She Said

"Zero complaints filed against this board have been upheld by the Attorney General, the Bureau of Consumer Protection, or any legal authority. Not one. Every single one was reviewed, responded to, and rejected."

What the Official Record Shows
Pennsylvania Attorney General — Public Records
There is no public database of HOA complaint outcomes maintained by the PA Attorney General's office. This claim cannot be independently verified by any resident.
She is asking you to accept an unverifiable claim as definitive fact. Residents have no way to confirm whether complaints were filed, how many, or what the outcomes were without a formal records request.
Why This Misses the Point
Filing a complaint creates an official paper trail, puts the board on notice, and documents the dispute — regardless of whether a formal finding is issued.
Most HOA complaints do not result in formal "upheld" findings — that is not how the process works. The AG's role is to receive complaints, investigate, and respond. "Rejected" and "reviewed and responded to" are not the same thing. Residents have the right to file complaints and should.
⚡ Ask Yourself
She is asking you to trust a claim about confidential government complaint records that no resident can verify. The appropriate response is: provide documentation, or residents will continue to exercise their legal right to file.
