Misleading

"The prior security contract cost approximately $250,000 annually and provided limited effectiveness (no enforcement capability or patrol)."

February 25, 2026 — BMLA Public Session Minutes
"The proposed budget for a new security plan is $45,250."
She says money was "redirected toward real measures." The record shows it was redirected to a $45,250 budget with no implemented plan — one-fifth of what was previously spent, still unspent as of June.
⚡ The Question She Doesn't Answer
Calling the old contract worthless is a defense of the past. It doesn't answer what residents do right now — 90+ days into summer with no security presence of any kind. What are the "real measures" and where are they?

Her Claim: "Zero Complaints Have Been Upheld"
Unverifiable
▼
What She Said

"Zero complaints filed against this board have been upheld by the Attorney General, the Bureau of Consumer Protection, or any legal authority. Not one. Every single one was reviewed, responded to, and rejected."

What the Official Record Shows
Pennsylvania Attorney General — Public Records
There is no public database of HOA complaint outcomes maintained by the PA Attorney General's office. This claim cannot be independently verified by any resident.
She is asking residents to accept an unverifiable claim as definitive fact. No resident can confirm whether complaints were filed, how many, or what the outcomes were without a formal records request.
Why This Misses the Point
"Rejected" and "reviewed and responded to" are not the same thing.
Most HOA complaints do not result in formal "upheld" findings — that is not how the process works. Filing creates an official paper trail and puts the board on notice, regardless of outcome. Residents have the right to file and should.
⚡ Ask Yourself
She is asking residents to trust a claim about confidential government complaint records that no resident can verify. The appropriate response: provide documentation, or residents will continue to exercise their legal right to file.

She says she's been fighting for homeowners on the QH issue. The official record shows QH wrote off $108,637 in 2024 alone and paid $102,500 less in 2025 under a new agreement she approved. Every BMLA homeowner absorbs that difference in their dues.
