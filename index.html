Governance

Structure & documents

BMLA is governed under the Pennsylvania Uniform Planned Community Act. The Board has six seats: three elected by Unit 1 owners (2-year terms) and three appointed by Quail Hollow Village.

The Quail Hollow Voting Issue: QHV holds 54 residential units but controls the majority of votes in community-wide decisions. In the 2024 covenant extension, QHV cast 2,808 of approximately 4,462 total yes votes — making their participation effectively required for any decision requiring a two-thirds supermajority. Residents cannot separate from QHV without their agreement. The 2025 Agreement commits BMLA to seek a Declaration amendment that would restructure QHV's obligations — but any amendment still requires a community-wide membership vote.
